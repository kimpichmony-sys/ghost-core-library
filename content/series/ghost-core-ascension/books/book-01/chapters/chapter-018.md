The first strike came before the dawn bell finished trembling.

Jin Ryu had not even completed his first breath.

Mu Sanchul moved like an apology.

There was no shout. No dramatic stomp. No killing intent strong enough to make the weaker disciples in the crowd gasp and step back. He simply bowed, lowered his eyes as though embarrassed to trouble a junior, and let his right foot slide half an inch through the cold arena sand.

To most of the Outer Yard, it looked like nothing.

To Jin, three things happened at once.

The sand beside Mu's left heel folded inward.

The loose sleeve around Mu's right wrist dipped too slowly.

The tendons in Mu's throat tightened before his shoulders moved.

**Incoming.**

Not a punch.

Mu's hand flicked toward Jin's face with two fingers extended, gentle as a physician checking fever.

Jin leaned back.

Too late.

The two fingers grazed his lower lip.

A hot sting burst across his mouth.

The crowd laughed.

"Already?" someone shouted. "He bled from a greeting!"

Jin staggered two steps and pressed the back of his wrist to his mouth. His heart kicked against his ribs hard enough to shake the fever patch behind his left ear.

No wetness.

Only pain.

Seolha's blue lotus salt burned beneath his tongue where she had hidden it before dawn. Mu's fingers had torn the skin, but the salt and ash mixture clotted the cut almost instantly, coating his mouth with a bitter, medicinal numbness.

**Oral blood collection attempt failed.**

Jin lowered his hand.

Mu Sanchul smiled with polite concern.

"Forgive me," he said. "Your guard was lower than expected."

Jin bowed his head, still bent from the retreat.

"Senior Brother Mu is quick."

Weak.

Respectful.

Almost grateful.

The crowd liked that. A weak man who knew he was weak did not threaten the order of the world.

That was the mask Jin had survived inside for ten years.

At the edge of the arena, Ma Doksu cupped his hands around his mouth.

"Jin! If he touches your face again, at least thank him for checking whether you still have one!"

Laughter rolled across the square.

Jin let it pass over him.

Mockery was fog.

Fog hid sharp things.

The Arena Clerk, standing beneath the witness awning with his scroll clutched in both hands, cleared his throat. "First exchange recorded. Combat correction assistant initiated contact. Subject maintained standing posture. No physician stop invoked. Continue."

No physician stop.

Seolha's hand remained closed around the single marker stone at her side.

Her face showed nothing.

But Jin saw the smallest shift in her sleeve.

Left ear.

She was warning him.

Mu's next strike came lower.

He stepped in with a short kick aimed not at Jin's knee, but at the soft hollow beside it. A beautiful target. A cruel target. The kind of place that did not break bone, only made the leg forget that standing was its duty.

**Recommendation: retreat forty-two centimeters.**

No.

Forty-two centimeters was a normal disciple's retreat.

Jin's meridians were sealed. His right calf still trembled from Black Needle Cliff. His breath was shallow after two nights of fever. If he retreated that far, his heel would drag. His balance would show calculation. Mu would adjust.

Jin retreated nineteen centimeters.

Ugly.

Awkward.

His shoe scraped the sand and caught a ridge left by the first exchange. Mu's foot hissed past his knee and struck the loose cloth of his trouser instead of flesh.

The crowd groaned, disappointed.

Mu's eyes narrowed by one thread.

That was the first true victory of the morning.

Not avoiding the kick.

Making Mu wonder whether Jin had avoided it on purpose.

Mu followed with a palm toward Jin's chest. Not hard enough to shatter ribs. Not soft enough to be harmless. If it landed clean, the shock would travel through the sternum, disturb the breath, and make any hidden pulse irregular.

Physician Lu stood behind Kang Taejun with his hands tucked into his sleeves.

He was not watching Jin's chest.

He was watching the space behind Jin's left ear.

Jin twisted late.

The palm struck his shoulder instead.

Pain exploded white and simple.

Jin fell.

He did not roll like a trained fighter. He did not use Broken Star Step to turn collapse into movement. He hit the sand shoulder-first and let the impact travel wrong through his body.

His teeth clicked.

His vision flickered.

The fever patch behind his ear remained untouched.

**Fall damage suboptimal.**

Owned patterns only, Jin thought.

The Ghost Core quieted.

Mu Sanchul looked down at him with that same mild smile.

"Junior Brother Jin," he said, "do you require rest?"

The words were kind.

His foot was already shifting toward Jin's head.

If Jin accepted help, Mu would extend a hand. Fingertips near wrist. Pulse contact. Blood pressure mapping. Maybe a needle hidden beneath the nail. If Jin refused, Mu would be allowed to pressure him for failing to comply with correction.

Jin coughed and pushed himself up badly.

Not too fast.

Not too slow.

A servant disciple who had carried water for years knew how to stand with bruised shoulders.

He stood like that.

The clerk made another mark on the scroll. "Subject recovered without aid. Second exchange continues."

A faint murmur moved through the senior disciples.

Not admiration.

Irritation.

A weak man was supposed to fall in a way that simplified paperwork.

Jin breathed through the taste of bitter lotus salt and old blood. His lower lip throbbed. His shoulder burned. His knee felt hollow. The world had become a net of small pains, each tugging for attention.

The Ghost Core displayed a dim interface at the back of his mind.

**Current constraints:**

**No full reconstruction.**

**No motor override.**

**No emotional suppression.**

**No direct scan through unknown Directorate observer.**

**Available assets:**

**Sand ridge pattern.**

**Opponent tendon delay.**

**Fever patch interference.**

**Public mockery.**

**One physician stop.**

Jin almost laughed.

The list sounded pathetic.

Then again, so did he.

Mu attacked again.

This time he did not hide the speed.

His loose sleeve snapped forward, and a thin line of silver flashed at the cuff.

Not a blade.

A wire.

So fine it was almost invisible in dawn light.

The wire curved toward Jin's cheek.

If it kissed skin, it would open a shallow cut. Not enough to count as injury. Enough to make blood fall into sand. Enough for an arena attendant to collect later while sweeping.

Jin stepped forward.

The crowd gasped.

It was the wrong direction.

Mu's wire passed behind Jin's head instead of across his face, and Jin's forehead nearly struck Mu's chest.

Nearly.

Jin bent lower at the last moment, clumsy as if his legs had failed. His shoulder bumped Mu's ribs with the force of a tired mule leaning against a gate.

Mu did not move.

But his breath hitched.

Only once.

**Old tendon injury creates right-side guarding compensation. Rib spacing altered.**

Jin ignored the line.

He did not counter.

He did not touch Mu with his fingers.

He stumbled away holding his own shoulder as if embarrassed.

"Sorry," he muttered.

The word carried just far enough for the first row to hear.

More laughter.

"He apologized for attacking!"

"That was not attacking. That was falling sideways!"

Doksu's voice rose above them. "That is his special technique! Cowardly Mountain Goat Bump! Rare in three provinces!"

Even a few Iron Moon disciples snorted.

Kang did not laugh.

Neither did Seo Yiran.

Seo stood slightly behind Kang, arms folded inside pale-gray sleeves, her expression calm enough to be mistaken for boredom. Her gaze moved from Jin's feet to Mu's sleeve, then to the clerk's scroll, then to Physician Lu.

She was not watching the fight.

She was watching the trap.

Jin wished she would be less intelligent.

Mu's smile thinned.

"Junior Brother Jin," he said softly, "your recovery step is unusual. Who taught you?"

Jin rubbed sand from his sleeve.

"Falling down taught me most things, Senior Brother."

The crowd laughed again.

Baek, leaning under the old cedar at the arena's edge with a wine gourd hanging from one hand, barked louder than all of them.

"Then you should be a master by now!"

Jin lowered his head.

Inside, his breath settled.

Baek's insult was instruction.

Do not look proud.

Do not look trained.

Let them think pain was your teacher.

Mu shifted stance.

The old tendon injury in his right sleeve changed the shape of his attacks. He could strike fast with that arm, but not with full extension. The hidden wire gave him reach. The footwork compensated by placing his left foot slightly deeper before every serious attempt to target Jin's head or ear.

Ghost Core could have drawn the pattern perfectly.

Jin did not ask it to.

He watched.

Foot.

Hip.

Shoulder.

Sleeve.

Breath.

Pain made the world narrow. Fear made it shake. Training made it usable.

Mu came in with three strikes.

Palm to chest.

Elbow to jaw.

Wire toward ear.

Jin failed beautifully.

He blocked the palm with the wrong forearm, letting the force bruise flesh but not crack bone. He ducked the elbow too late, so it clipped his temple and sent bright sparks across his vision. He twisted away from the wire by pretending the elbow had stunned him worse than it had.

The wire sliced through the edge of the fever blister patch.

Not skin.

Patch.

A tiny strip of ash-treated paste fluttered into the sand.

Physician Lu's eyes sharpened.

Seolha's fingers tightened around the marker stone.

Jin's stomach dropped.

Mu had not drawn blood, but he had cut the cover.

The implant mark behind Jin's left ear prickled with cold heat.

**Interface zone exposure risk increased.**

Jin staggered backward and slapped a hand to the side of his neck, as if checking the bruise from the elbow.

His palm covered the damaged patch.

Mu saw it.

So did Lu.

So did Kang.

Kang smiled for the first time that morning.

"Enough crawling," Kang called from the witness side. "Mu Sanchul, correct his guard properly. If a disciple hides weakness behind clumsiness, he dishonors the assessment."

The clerk opened his mouth, then closed it.

He did not want to argue with Kang in public.

Seolha spoke before Baek could.

"Combat correction assistant Mu has already targeted the subject's face and head repeatedly," she said. "Further head contact risks invalidating medical observation."

Physician Lu smiled gently. "Physician Han is protective. Admirable. But the arena exists to reveal whether a disciple can protect himself. If every bruise becomes a medical objection, the assessment loses meaning."

"A bruise is not my objection," Seolha said.

Her voice remained calm.

That made it colder.

"A pattern is."

A small quiet fell.

The clerk looked between them like a man watching two snakes decide whether his boot was a neutral territory.

Mu bowed toward Seolha. "I will be more careful."

He would not.

Everyone knew it.

The clerk swallowed and raised his hand. "Third exchange. Continue under caution."

Third exchange.

The assessment needed only three official exchanges to produce a result.

If Jin remained standing until the clerk closed the record, the arena could not declare him incapable without further petition. If he bled cleanly, the Directorate won. If he exposed unnatural movement, the Directorate won. If Seolha used her stop too early, Kang could demand a second assessment under another physician.

Three exchanges should have been short.

Politics made them longer than winter.

Mu's courtesy vanished.

He stepped forward with his left foot deep.

Wire attack.

No.

Feint.

His right hand lifted, sleeve loose, but his true strike rose from below: two fingers toward the soft point under Jin's jaw.

A pressure touch there would numb the tongue and force saliva from the mouth.

Blue lotus salt. Blood. Contamination.

Maybe enough for Lu.

Jin did not retreat.

He inhaled.

Cork training.

Baek's disgusting wine breath. Dawn mist. Teeth aching around a cork until the jaw learned obedience. Breath through nose. Tongue flat. Throat closed. Do not let the body panic just because someone else gave it a reason.

Mu's fingers struck under Jin's jaw.

Pain shot into his ears.

His tongue went numb.

His knees buckled.

But his mouth stayed closed.

A wet sound tried to rise from his throat.

He swallowed it.

The salt burned like fire.

Mu's eyes flickered.

Jin let both knees fail.

He dropped straight down.

Mu's hidden wire, already curving toward the left side of Jin's head for the follow-up, passed above him and cut only air.

Jin hit the sand on one knee, one hand braced, head low.

In that position, he looked defeated.

He was also inside Mu's stance.

Do not be brilliant.

Do not be beautiful.

Do not be a monster.

Be a stone in a shoe.

Jin shifted his braced hand and scooped sand into Mu's left ankle.

Not threw.

Scooped.

A child's motion. An accident. A desperate attempt to stand that dragged grit beneath Mu's heel.

Mu adjusted.

The old tendon injury forced his right leg to compensate.

His balance crossed for half a breath.

Jin stood into him.

Not a strike.

Not a technique.

A servant rising with a bucket on one shoulder.

His bruised shoulder drove into Mu's centerline again, exactly where the rib spacing had changed.

This time Mu took one step back.

Only one.

But the arena saw it.

The laughter died strangely.

A servant disciple had moved the correction assistant.

Jin immediately ruined the moment.

He tripped over his own foot and fell backward on his backside.

Doksu shouted, "Aha! See? The mountain goat has a second form!"

The laughter returned, but thinner.

Mu's face remained polite.

His eyes did not.

The clerk's brush hovered.

"Third exchange... subject maintained... partial counter-contact... assistant displaced one step..."

The Arena Clerk sounded as if each word might get him stabbed later.

Kang's jaw tightened.

Physician Lu leaned forward slightly.

Seo Yiran's gaze sharpened with quiet interest.

Jin sat in the sand and breathed through his nose. His jaw ached. His shoulder screamed. The damaged fever patch clung behind his ear in torn pieces beneath his covering palm.

**Assessment objective nearly achieved.**

Not yet.

Mu Sanchul looked at the clerk.

Then at Kang.

Then at Jin.

"Clerk," Mu said, voice still mild, "the subject has not demonstrated offensive correction response. If he is to remain listed for Outer Yard ranking review, he must show whether his movement can threaten an opponent at all. Otherwise, this assessment only proves he can fall creatively."

The crowd murmured.

That was not a normal request.

But it sounded reasonable.

The most dangerous traps always did.

Kang said, "Iron Moon Hall supports the request. A disciple who survived the Thousand-Step Trial should not fear demonstrating one clean attack."

Seolha's eyes went to Jin.

Do not.

She did not say it.

She did not need to.

A clean attack meant commitment. Commitment meant force. Force meant pulse change. Pulse change meant the Ghost Core would either assist or Jin's body would fail. Either way, Lu would watch.

Baek drank from his gourd.

Then spat wine into the dirt.

"A clean attack? From that boy? If he had one, he would have used it to steal an extra steamed bun years ago."

More laughter, but the political current had shifted.

The clerk looked trapped.

"Subject Jin Ryu," he said reluctantly, "can you demonstrate one controlled offensive movement?"

Jin lowered his hand from his ear very carefully.

The patch stayed in place by luck and Seolha's paste.

He stood.

His legs did not want to.

He bowed to the clerk.

"I can try."

Weak answer.

Safe answer.

Mu smiled again.

He opened his arms slightly, inviting the attack.

A generous senior giving a broken junior a chance to embarrass himself.

Jin saw the trap inside the open posture.

Mu's left foot was forward. Right sleeve loose. Chin exposed by one finger's width. Chest open. A dozen tempting targets.

Every one of them was watched.

If Jin attacked the chin, Mu would cut his wrist with the wire.

If Jin attacked the chest, Mu would capture his elbow and map his pulse.

If Jin attacked the leg, Mu would collapse onto him and open the fever patch.

If Jin did nothing, the assessment would mark him incapable.

Ghost Core offered no recommendation.

Good.

Jin did not need a perfect answer.

He needed an ugly one that belonged to him.

He thought of Black Needle Cliff.

Step where death is thinner.

He thought of the cork between his teeth.

Breathe through insult.

He thought of Seolha's needles.

Do not confuse the patient with the secret inside the patient.

He thought of Doksu's loud foolish loyalty.

Make the crowd laugh before they fear you.

He thought of Aera's voice, almost lost and almost found.

I did not break you because I hated you.

Jin exhaled.

Then he attacked the one thing no one expected him to attack.

The arena rule.

He stepped forward badly, raised both hands as if to shove Mu's chest, then stopped half a handspan short.

Mu reacted anyway.

His wire hand twitched.

His left foot rooted.

His ribs guarded.

The crowd saw nothing.

The clerk saw Jin stop.

Physician Lu saw Mu react.

Seolha saw Jin's eyes.

Jin let his knees buckle again.

As he fell, his right hand brushed the sand where Mu's left foot had rooted. Two fingers touched the grit beneath the heel. Not Mu. Not skin. Not pulse.

Sand.

Then Jin's collapsing shoulder bumped Mu's knee from the outside.

Barely.

A child could have done more damage kicking a stool.

But Mu's weight was already committed to an attack he had not been allowed to launch.

The sand under his heel shifted.

His knee bent inward.

His old tendon injury stole the correction.

Mu Sanchul's balance broke.

For the first time that morning, the correction assistant fell.

Not far.

Not dramatically.

He dropped to one hand and one knee, sleeve snapping down to hide the silver wire.

Silence struck the arena harder than any fist.

Jin was also on the ground, sprawled like a fool who had collapsed during his own attack.

But Mu's knee touched sand first.

The clerk stared.

His brush dripped ink onto the scroll.

Doksu's mouth opened.

For once, no joke came out.

Baek's wine gourd stopped halfway to his lips.

Seolha closed her eyes for half a heartbeat.

Kang stood.

That movement broke the silence.

"Invalid," Kang said.

His voice did not rise.

It did not need to.

"The subject fell. That was not an attack."

The clerk flinched. "The correction assistant lost balance during—"

"During a stumble. Are we now recording accidents as skill?"

Physician Lu smiled softly. "A reasonable question. However, the assessment requested controlled offensive movement. Intent may be difficult to establish."

Jin stayed on the sand and did not smile.

Inside his skull, the Ghost Core pulsed once.

**Opponent destabilized without direct host signature exposure.**

Do not celebrate.

**Agreement.**

Mu rose slowly.

The polite mask had cracked at the corners.

A thin line of red appeared on his own palm where the hidden wire had pressed too hard when he caught himself.

His blood fell into the sand.

Not Jin's.

The Skyless Directorate wanted blood.

It had received the wrong man's.

"Correct. Blood contamination on platform. Third exchange concluded. Sand replacement required. Subject demonstrated standing recovery, defensive response, and... partial offensive displacement."

Kang's stare could have cracked pottery.

"Clerk."

But the words were already moving, and procedure once spoken in public became harder to bury.

"Preliminary result," the clerk said quickly, "subject Jin Ryu is not suitable for immediate removal from Outer Yard ranking review. Combat capability remains low but not null. Medical observation to continue under Moonlit Medicine Pavilion authority pending final elder stamp."

Low but not null.

A pathetic result.

A miraculous one.

Jin lowered his head until his forehead nearly touched the sand.

The crowd erupted in argument.

Some laughed. Some complained. Some demanded a rematch. Some insisted Mu had slipped. Some insisted the Broken Pulse Dog had used demonic luck. A few servant disciples looked at Jin with something dangerous and shining in their eyes.

Hope.

Jin wished they would stop.

Hope made people careless.

Mu Sanchul wrapped his bleeding palm inside his sleeve and bowed to the clerk.

"I accept the preliminary record," he said.

His voice was calm again.

Too calm.

Then he turned to Jin.

"Junior Brother Jin. Your survival is impressive."

Jin bowed from the ground.

"Senior Brother Mu was merciful."

Mu leaned closer as if helping him up.

Jin did not take the hand.

Mu's smile deepened.

In a voice too soft for the crowd, he said, "You know how to avoid giving blood. Host-02 knew that too, near the end."

Cold slid through Jin's spine.

The Ghost Core's interface flashed and vanished.

**Host-02 reference confirmed from combat assistant. Threat level—**

Minimum scan.

The line dimmed.

Mu straightened before anyone could notice.

Seolha stepped into the arena with physician authority before Jin tried to stand alone.

"Subject is under medical follow-up," she said.

She knelt beside him and placed two fingers near his wrist.

Not on the pulse point.

Beside it.

A lie for the watchers.

Her other hand moved to the torn patch behind his ear.

"Do not react," she murmured.

Jin stared at the sand.

Seolha pressed fresh paste over the damaged edge in one smooth motion, using her sleeve to hide the movement from Lu.

The paste burned.

Jin's jaw tightened.

"You disobeyed half my plan," she whispered.

"Only half?"

"Do not make jokes when I am deciding whether to save you or poison you myself."

"Noted."

Her fingers trembled once.

Only once.

Then she stood and raised the marker stone without breaking it.

"Medical observation continues. No stop invoked."

The crowd murmured louder.

No stop invoked meant Jin had completed the assessment without consuming her one formal protection.

That mattered.

It mattered too much.

Every disciple near his path moved aside.

He wore pale martial robes edged with iron-gray thread, clean enough to insult everyone who had bled that morning. His hair was tied perfectly. His face carried the calm of someone who had never been hungry long enough to learn humility.

He stopped at the arena boundary and looked at the clerk.

"Outer Yard Procedure Office records combat capability as low but not null," Kang said.

The clerk swallowed. "Preliminarily, pending elder stamp."

"Then the subject may receive formal correction challenges."

Baek pushed himself off the cedar.

"Careful, little iron moon," he said. "Your robe is too clean for the mud you are stepping into."

Kang ignored him.

He looked at Jin.

For the first time since the Thousand-Step Trial, Kang did not look amused.

He looked inconvenienced.

That was worse.

"Jin Ryu," Kang said, voice carrying across the square, "you have been treated with patience beyond your station. You survived the Thousand-Step Trial. You disturbed an official banquet. You turned a correction assessment into farce. Now Procedure Office has recorded that you possess measurable combat response."

The crowd quieted again.

Kang smiled without warmth.

"As senior disciple of Iron Moon Hall, I petition for a formal rank correction bout."

Seolha's face hardened.

Doksu cursed under his breath.

The clerk's shoulders sagged with despair.

"Senior Disciple Kang," he said, "rank correction bouts require equivalent category or approved handicap—"

It sounded generous.

It was murder dressed as etiquette.

Kang did not need internal force to cripple Jin.

He only needed one clean contact.

Physician Lu smiled.

Seo Yiran's eyes moved to Kang with something like warning.

"Brother Kang," she said quietly, but he did not look at her.

Baek's voice cut across the arena.

"Petition denied. The boy just finished assessment."

Kang finally turned his head.

"Master Baek has no hall authority here."

The old man's grin showed yellow teeth.

"No. But I have eyes. Your petition stinks."

Several disciples inhaled sharply.

Kang's expression did not change.

"Then let the clerk follow procedure."

The clerk looked as if he might faint.

Procedure.

Again.

Jin hated that word.

The Skyless Directorate used poison and needles. Kang used rules. Both worked because the sect pretended paper was cleaner than blood.

"A formal rank correction petition may be lodged immediately following assessment if the assessed subject receives a non-null combat record," he recited. "However, if subject is under medical observation, attending physician may request delay until first bell after noon. If petitioner alleges public disorder risk, delay may be shortened by witness vote."

Kang's smile returned.

"Public disorder risk."

He gestured toward the crowd.

"The Outer Yard is already confused. Some believe a servant disciple humiliated a correction assistant. Others believe Iron Moon Hall manipulated assessment. A clean bout will settle rumor."

He was clever.

Too clever.

If Seolha demanded delay, Kang would accuse her of protecting Jin. If Baek interfered, Kang would frame it as elder bias. If Jin refused, he lost the record and looked like a coward who had survived by accident.

If Jin accepted, he would face Kang while injured, watched by Lu, with blood still one mistake away.

**Recommended refusal. Survival probability increases by—**

No.

**Host condition unstable.**

I know.

**Kang Taejun combat profile incomplete.**

I know.

**Formal bout creates high probability of blood exposure.**

I know.

For years, Kang had been a wall in the shape of a person. Not because he was the strongest in the world. Not even because he was the strongest in the sect. Because in Jin's small broken life, Kang decided where pain came from and when it stopped.

A wall did not need hatred to crush a worm.

It only needed to stand where the worm tried to crawl.

His shoulder burned. His jaw throbbed. The patch behind his ear stung. His stomach twisted from medicine and fear. Every part of him wanted to return to the Medicine Pavilion, drink whatever bitter thing Seolha forced into his mouth, and sleep until the world forgot his name.

"Jin," she said softly.

There was also understanding.

"And if I remain standing after thirty breaths, I keep the record?"

Kang's eyes sharpened.

"Senior Brother Kang is generous."

Doksu whispered, "Oh no. I know that idiot tone."

His face remained humble.

Baek made a sound suspiciously close to choking on laughter.

The clerk stared at Jin with sudden, desperate gratitude. "That is... procedurally clean."

Kang studied him.

Jin lowered his gaze.

"This junior's breathing is unreliable. I would not want Senior Brother Kang's generosity misunderstood."

Generosity.

He wrapped the chain around Kang's own word.

Kang could refuse, but refusing made him look like he wanted a flexible count. Accepting forced him into a public measure.

Physician Lu's smile faded slightly.

Seo Yiran looked at Jin for a long moment, then lowered her eyes as if hiding a thought.

"Agreed. Three incense lines. No weapons. No internal force beyond first circulation. Ring boundary. Physician present. If you remain standing when the third line dies, you keep your pathetic record. If you fall, withdraw, bleed sufficiently for medical stop, or leave the ring, you lose."

Bleed sufficiently.

There it was.

His eyes smiled.

His mouth did not.

Kang said, "Noon."

Seolha said, "First bell after noon. Medical observation requires minimum interval."

Kang's gaze flicked to her.

"First bell after noon," he agreed.

Three hours to repair a body that had been broken for years.

Three hours to prepare for Kang Taejun.

Three hours before Jin had to stand in the same arena and survive the person who had named him Broken Pulse Dog.

"Senior Brother is considerate."

As he passed Physician Lu, their sleeves brushed.

Jin almost missed it.

Almost.

**Visual anomaly detected. Sample transfer probable.**

Whose sample?

**Unknown. Mu Sanchul blood confirmed present. Environmental contamination possible. Host material probability low but nonzero.**

Seolha saw his face and followed his gaze.

Her expression did not change, but something behind her eyes sharpened like a needle drawn from silk.

It hurt.

"He did not kiss it," Jin said.
