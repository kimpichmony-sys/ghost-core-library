Jin Ryu woke to the sound of law being sharpened.

Not steel.

Not saber edges drawn across whetstone.

Law.

Brush bristles scratching paper. Seals being pressed into red paste. Wooden tablets tapped against a table in three short strikes, each strike dry and official enough to make the air inside the Moonlit Medicine Pavilion feel colder than medicine should allow.

His body understood pain before his mind understood place.

Left knee: swollen.

Right shoulder: bruised to the bone.

Ribs: cracked or pretending not to be.

Jaw: stiff from Mu Sanchul's sleeve-wire kiss.

Behind the left ear: heat beneath the fever blister patch, slow and buried, as if a coal had been stitched under his skin and told to behave.

Then came the Ghost Core.

**Consciousness restored.**

**Motor recovery: partial.**

**Knee stability: insufficient for combat.**

**Neural strain: severe.**

**Recommendation: remain silent, remain horizontal, remain underestimated.**

Jin would have laughed if his throat had not tasted of old blood.

That was the first thing he noticed.

The taste.

Not clean blood. Seolha had been careful. Blue lotus salt, fever ash, sour resin, and two bitter medicines he had swallowed while half-conscious had turned his mouth into a ruined herb jar. Any drop taken from his lips after the fight would read like the bottom of a medicine pot.

Not clean.

Never clean.

The Skyless Directorate wanted clean things. Clean pulse maps. Clean blood. Clean obedience.

Jin was beginning to like dirt.

He forced his eyes open.

The ceiling of the pavilion drifted above him, pale beams crossing like old bones under skin. Paper charms hung from the rafters. Most were medicine talismans: warming, cooling, stopping blood, easing fever. One was not. One had been drawn badly on purpose, the crooked line hiding a needle-thin strip of black metal inside the folded paper.

Baek's work.

A blind man's warning, pinned where only the wrong kind of watcher would bother to look.

Seolha stood near the table by the door, sleeves tied back, hair fastened with her plain silver pin. She looked calm in the way snow looked calm before it buried a road. Beside her, Ma Doksu sat on a stool with one arm in a sling, cheeks puffed around a mouthful of medicine cake, glaring at the room as if daring it to harm anyone else.

Master Baek lay across the visitor bench with one sandal dangling from his toes, face turned toward the ceiling, blind eyes covered by a strip of faded cloth. He looked asleep.

His fingers were awake.

They tapped the bench once.

Then twice.

Then stopped.

Two people outside the door.

Jin listened.

The brush scratching stopped.

A male voice spoke, oily with effortful respect.

"Healer Han, by order of Elder Jang and the Discipline Hall, the patient Jin Ryu is summoned for immediate elder review. His continued absence will be considered refusal to answer accusations."

Seolha did not turn.

"My patient cannot walk."

"He stood through three incense lines."

"Because I stopped the match before his knee collapsed completely."

"Then carry him."

Doksu swallowed his medicine cake too fast and coughed. "Carry him? Are your ancestors embarrassed of you yet?"

The man outside ignored him. "The petition has been filed. Kang Taejun claims procedural irregularity, deliberate physician interference, crowd manipulation, foreign medicine contamination, and concealment of martial capacity."

Baek's mouth curved.

"Foreign medicine contamination," he murmured. "That one has teeth. Tiny little baby teeth."

Seolha looked at Jin.

Her eyes asked a question she did not speak in front of the door.

Can you survive being seen?

Jin's first instinct was to ask the Ghost Core.

The thought rose fast and sharp.

Calculate route. Calculate testimony. Calculate Kang. Calculate Seolha's safest lie. Calculate the most efficient arrangement of truths.

The coal behind his ear pulsed.

Something deeper than the Ghost Core stirred in the dark beneath his skull.

A door.

Smoke.

The memory of a warning.

**Host. Do not answer if it speaks.**

Jin closed his eyes once.

Not fear.

Not surrender.

A choice.

Minimum scan.

No memory access.

No emotional suppression.

No speaking to the thing behind the door.

The Ghost Core answered with unusual softness.

**Restrictions accepted.**

**Current host authority recognized.**

Current.

That word sat in his mind like a thorn.

Jin pushed it aside.

He raised one hand.

The fingers trembled.

"I can go," he rasped.

Doksu lurched up. "You can go into a grave too if someone throws you hard enough. Doesn't mean it's a good travel plan."

Seolha crossed the room and pressed two fingers below Jin's jaw. Her touch was cool. Professional. Too steady.

"No walking," she said.

"If they carry me like a corpse, Kang wins before we arrive."

"If you walk, your knee may not forgive you."

"My knee has been rude to me since sunrise. We are not close friends."

Doksu made a wounded noise. "Do not joke with the healer. She has needles and no humor left."

Seolha's eyes narrowed at Jin, but the corner of her mouth moved by the width of a breath.

Then she said, "Three steps."

Jin blinked.

"From the bed to the chair. If you can take three steps without losing consciousness, I will let you appear upright. If you cannot, I will have you carried and I will enjoy watching Kang try to argue with a physician's chair."

Baek lifted one finger. "For record accuracy, I would also enjoy that."

Outside the door, the clerk cleared his throat loudly. "Healer Han, delay will be noted."

Seolha's voice became colder. "Note also that Discipline Hall has now interrupted emergency treatment twice in one day. If my patient dies, write your own name clearly so I know which family to send the bill to."

Silence outside.

Doksu whispered, "She does have humor. It just wears knives."

Jin sat up.

The room tilted.

The Ghost Core filled his vision with pale lines marking balance, pressure, weight distribution, pain thresholds, fall directions. Jin dismissed half of them. Too many lines made the world look like a cage.

He put his feet on the floor.

Cold wood.

His left knee screamed.

Not a metaphor. Something inside it truly seemed to open a mouth.

Seolha's hand hovered near his elbow but did not touch. Doksu stood on the other side, pretending not to be ready to catch him. Baek remained sprawled on the bench, smiling like an evil ancestor waiting to see whether his descendant would embarrass him.

Jin took one step.

His body tried to fold.

He breathed around it.

Cork breath. Cliff breath. Doorstop breath.

Step where death is thinner.

Second step.

The knee bent wrong for half a heartbeat.

Ghost Core offered correction.

Jin rejected full correction.

He moved ugly instead.

Crooked instead.

Alive instead.

Third step.

He reached the chair, gripped the back, and swallowed the blackness climbing his vision.

No one spoke.

Then Baek sighed. "Terrible. Absolutely terrible. If stubbornness were footwork, you would already be an elder."

Seolha tied a fresh band around Jin's knee with movements so precise they felt angry.

"You will not fight."

"I know."

"You will not demonstrate."

"I know."

"You will not let Kang make you bleed."

Jin looked toward the door.

Beyond it, the official men waited with their brushes, seals, and laws sharpened into little knives.

"Then I should not bite him?"

Doksu looked horrified. "Why would you say that in front of witnesses?"

Baek sat up.

His cloth-covered eyes turned toward Jin.

"Sometimes," the old man said, "the dog does not need teeth. Sometimes he only needs to drag the moon low enough for everyone to see the cracks."

Seolha finished the knot.

"Remember that. Procedure is not justice. But if someone worships procedure, let him kneel before it first."

Jin stood.

This time, Seolha did not stop him.

She put a physician's walking staff in his hand instead.

It was plain white wood. Too straight. Too smooth.

A healer's tool.

A patient support.

A symbol that said he was injured under official medical protection.

A weapon, if held by someone petty enough to use weakness beautifully.

Baek's grin widened.

"Ah," he said. "Now the teeth show."


## The Hall That Ate Voices

The Elder Review Hall had been built to make people feel small.

It sat behind the Discipline Hall, halfway between the Outer Yard and the inner terraces, with stone lion statues guarding its steps and iron wind bells hanging beneath its eaves. The bells did not ring easily. The sect claimed they sounded only when the mountain's breath judged a trial important.

Doksu claimed they sounded only when an elder kicked the wall behind them.

That morning, they were silent.

The courtyard was not.

Disciples had gathered in layers. Outer disciples near the front, kitchen servants pressed against the side walls, Iron Moon Hall youths standing together in clean robes and ugly pride. The dawn arena had already fed them spectacle. Now the review promised something rarer: consequences.

When Jin appeared at the lower steps, leaning on Seolha's white staff, the courtyard changed its breathing.

He felt it.

Not qi. Not yet. He barely had enough internal circulation to keep his blood from pooling where Kang had kicked him.

But attention had weight.

Yesterday, attention had stepped on him.

Today, it parted.

Not with respect.

Not yet.

But with uncertainty.

Broken Pulse Dog had climbed the Thousand-Step Trial.

Broken Pulse Dog had survived Mu Sanchul.

Broken Pulse Dog had stood through Kang Taejun's three incense lines.

A dog who refused to die became inconvenient.

Kang stood near the hall entrance in formal Iron Moon robes, hair tied high, cheek still marked by the faint dust and sweat of the afternoon bout. He looked clean because powerful people had attendants to wipe away struggle before others could study it.

But Jin saw the small things.

The tightness around Kang's jaw.

The stiffness in his right thumb.

The way he did not look at the staff first, but at Jin's mouth.

Blood.

Always blood.

Beside Kang waited Elder Jang, narrow-eyed and dry-faced, his beard combed to a point. Behind them stood the Arena Clerk, two Discipline scribes, and Physician Lu in his mild gray robe.

Lu's hands were empty.

That made Jin more afraid than if he had held a knife.

The Ghost Core flickered.

**Threat assessment: multiple hostile observers.**

**Primary risks: forced examination, blood verification, authority override, public credibility collapse.**

**Recommended posture: injured compliance.**

Injured compliance.

Jin almost smiled.

That sounded like something he had been trained for his entire life.

Seolha walked one step behind him and to the left, as custom allowed a physician escort. Doksu limped on the right, officially present as a witness to dawn arena events and unofficially present because separating him from trouble required more manpower than the sect currently wished to spend.

Baek did not walk with them.

He was already inside.

Of course he was.

The old man sat on the lowest guest mat, one hand inside his sleeve, head tilted as if listening to insects in the rafters. No one had invited him. No one seemed confident enough to remove him.

The review hall swallowed them.

Inside, elder tablets lined the walls. Black wood. Gold names. Dead men given better seats than living servants.

At the front sat three review elders.

Elder Jang took the right seat.

Elder Mo, keeper of Outer Yard registration, took the left, heavy-lidded and round as a winter jar.

The center seat belonged to Elder Sun Hae, an old woman with silver hair and hands folded so neatly they looked carved. Jin had seen her only once before, from behind a rice cart. She oversaw procedure disputes when the sect wanted to pretend fairness had not died completely.

Kang bowed deeply.

Jin bowed shallowly because his knee refused depth.

A murmur went through the hall.

Elder Jang's lip curled.

Seolha spoke before he could.

"Patient is under joint pavilion restriction. Bowing past half-angle risks tearing the stabilized knee. Record this as medical limitation, not disrespect."

A scribe wrote.

Scratch. Scratch. Scratch.

Elder Sun's gaze shifted to Seolha.

"Recorded."

Kang's expression did not change.

But Jin saw his thumb twitch.

One small point to them.

Elder Sun tapped the table once.

The room quieted.

"Review begins. Petitioner Kang Taejun of Iron Moon Hall claims the dawn assessment and subsequent rank correction bout were compromised by irregular procedure. State charges clearly."

Kang stepped forward.

His voice filled the hall with practiced restraint.

"Honored elders, I do not bring this petition from anger."

Doksu breathed through his nose in a way that sounded like a pig disagreeing.

Seolha's sleeve brushed his wrist. Warning.

Kang continued smoothly. "I bring it because sect rankings must remain clean. If weak disciples may hide ability, manipulate medicine, exploit physician bias, and force unclear timing measures, then the Outer Yard record becomes meaningless."

Elder Mo nodded faintly.

The Ghost Core marked him.

**Elder Mo: receptive to record-preservation argument.**

Jin did not need the note.

Men who guarded ledgers loved nothing more than being told ledgers were sacred.

Kang lifted one finger.

"First charge: Jin Ryu concealed martial capacity before entering the dawn assessment. His public reputation as meridian-broken influenced opponent conduct and crowd expectation."

Second finger.

"Second charge: Healer Han Seolha administered foreign medicines before and during the assessment, contaminating blood inspection and affecting endurance."

Third.

"Third charge: Ma Doksu interfered with the rank correction bout by throwing debris into the arena field."

Doksu opened his mouth.

Seolha's sleeve moved again.

He shut it with visible suffering.

Fourth finger.

"Fourth charge: the three-incense timing condition was accepted under crowd pressure and did not represent standard Outer Yard assessment terms."

Fifth finger.

Kang turned slightly, letting the hall see his face.

"Fifth charge: during the final incense line, Jin Ryu displayed unnatural movement for a disciple with his recorded condition. I request pulse verification, blood verification, and invalidation of today's results until his true capacity is examined."

There it was.

Everything dressed as fairness.

Everything pointed toward the same soft place behind Jin's ear.

Elder Jang leaned forward. "Reasonable."

Of course.

Elder Mo stroked his beard. "If the record is false, correction is necessary."

Elder Sun looked at Jin.

"Respondent may answer."

Seolha opened her mouth.

Jin lifted one finger from the staff.

Not to silence her.

To ask permission.

Her eyes narrowed.

Then she gave the smallest nod.

Jin stepped forward one half-step.

The pain turned white.

He let his face show some of it.

Not all.

Enough.

"Honored elders," he said, voice rough, "I am stupid and weak. I need help understanding the charges."

The hall shifted.

Kang's eyes sharpened.

Baek coughed into his sleeve.

Elder Sun did not smile.

"Ask."

Jin bowed his head. "First charge says I concealed martial capacity. Does the petitioner claim I am strong?"

Kang answered before the elders could. "I claim you are stronger than your record."

"So not strong."

"Do not twist words."

Jin looked at Elder Sun. "Please record that petitioner does not claim respondent is strong, only less weak than expected."

The scribe hesitated.

Elder Sun tapped once.

Scratch. Scratch.

Kang's jaw tightened.

Jin continued. "If a disciple improves through punishment, trial, medicine, and arena survival, is that concealment?"

Elder Mo frowned. "Depends on whether improvement was known and withheld from registration."

"My record was updated after the dawn assessment as low but not null. I did not write that record. The Arena Clerk did."

The Arena Clerk looked suddenly less comfortable.

Jin turned his head with difficulty.

"Clerk, did I ask you to record me as stronger?"

The clerk coughed. "No."

"Did Healer Han ask?"

"No."

"Did Ma Doksu throw a bun at you until you changed it?"

Doksu whispered, "I would never waste a bun."

Elder Sun's eyebrow moved.

The scribe's brush paused for half a heartbeat.

"Answer," Elder Sun said.

"No," the clerk said.

Jin faced forward. "Then the first charge is strange. The official record changed because officials watched me, not because I hid anything."

Kang stepped in. "You used unusual movement in the match with Mu Sanchul."

"I fell down."

"Strategically."

"I apologize for falling too cleverly."

A sound moved through the disciples packed near the hall doors.

Not laughter.

Not fully.

A dangerous almost-laughter.

Elder Jang slapped the table. "Mind your tone."

Jin lowered his eyes. "Recorded as fear, honored elder. Not disrespect."

Baek made a choking sound that might have been a dying cough or joy trying to escape.

Kang's face reddened by one shade.

The Ghost Core whispered:

**Social pressure shift: minor.**

**Petitioner irritation increasing.**

**Continue?**

Jin did not answer in words.

He only breathed.

Elder Sun said, "Proceed to second charge."

Kang looked relieved to reach firmer ground.

"Healer Han administered contaminating substances. The blood inspection after the assessment became unreliable. This suggests deliberate obstruction."

Seolha stepped forward.

Her bow was perfect.

"I administered blue lotus salt to stop oral bleeding, fever ash to reduce surface infection risk near an inflamed lesion behind the ear, tendon-stabilizing paste to the left knee, and bitter root infusion to prevent shock. All are listed in the Medicine Pavilion emergency manual."

Seolha looked at him.

The air between them chilled.

"A physician who recognizes anti-verification contamination so quickly must have encountered it often."

Lu smiled. "In imperial training, one studies many dangers."

"This is not the imperial capital. This is a sect review."

Elder Jang scowled. "Physician Lu has been invited as an outside medical witness."

"By whom?" Seolha asked.

Silence.

A tiny thing.

A thin crack.

Jin felt the whole hall lean toward it.

Elder Sun's gaze moved to Elder Jang.

"By whose seal was the outside medical witness invited?"

Elder Jang's face hardened. "Mine."

"For a routine Outer Yard arena review?"

"The irregularities warranted expertise."

Seolha bowed again, deeper. "Then please record that Elder Jang anticipated irregular blood-verification complications before the review began, despite no official blood charge being filed at the time the invitation seal was sent."

Scratch.

Scratch.

The scribe wrote because Elder Sun did not stop him.

Kang realized the ground had shifted.

"Healer Han is redirecting from her own contamination."

Seolha turned to him. "Did you request blood verification before the match?"

Kang hesitated.

"I requested fair assessment."

"Did you request blood verification before Mu Sanchul attempted to cut Jin Ryu's mouth?"

Kang's eyes flashed.

"Opponent technique is not the issue."

"Did you?"

"No."

"Then why was an outside physician prepared for blood verification?"

Physician Lu's smile remained.

But something behind it stopped being mild.

The Ghost Core wrote one small line in Jin's vision.

**Seolha counter-trap effective.**

Jin breathed carefully.

Pride warmed his chest.

He let himself feel it.

No emotional suppression.

"I am claiming my patient received mouth-targeting strikes, sleeve-wire laceration attempts, implant-area pressure, and repeated physician-side interest in blood sample reliability before any legitimate blood dispute had been established. I am claiming I treated injuries as they occurred. If my treatment interfered with unofficial collection, then unofficial collection should explain itself."

Doksu stared at Seolha with open admiration.

Baek whispered, "Knives."

Elder Jang snapped, "Implant-area?"

The word hit the room.

Too sharp.

Too dangerous.

Seolha did not blink.

"Inflamed lesion," she corrected. "Behind the left ear. Fever blister cluster caused by poison strain and overexertion. I used common shorthand."

Physician Lu looked at Jin's left ear.

Jin did not touch it.

The patch itched like a lie learning to sweat.

"Language will remain medically precise."

"Yes, honored elder," Seolha said.

Kang seized the third charge. "Ma Doksu interfered. Witnesses saw a pebble enter the ring during the correction bout. It affected my foot placement."

Doksu finally stepped forward.

His sling made his bow crooked.

"Honored elders, I did throw something."

Kang's satisfaction flashed bright.

Doksu continued, "It was not a pebble. It was a dried medicine cake crumb stuck to my sleeve. I sneezed because someone near me wore too much Iron Moon perfume. The crumb flew. I apologize to procedure for my nose."

A disciple near the door made a strangled sound.

Kang's nostrils flared.

"Lie."

Baek lifted a hand. "I can testify the object was not stone."

"Yes," Baek said pleasantly. "That is why I listen. Stone strikes sand differently from dried medicine cake. Also, the crowd debris record from the Arena Clerk notes six objects entered the outer boundary during the match: two straw bits, one sleeve bead, one sandal fiber, one dried crumb, and one insect. If Kang Taejun's footwork was defeated by a snack and a bug, perhaps his rank requires review."

The hall almost broke.

Almost.

Elder Sun's palm landed softly on the table.

Quiet returned with reluctance.

"Arena Clerk. Does your debris record contain those entries?"

"Yes, honored elder."

"Was any object marked as thrown weapon?"

"No, honored elder."

"Was any object marked as affecting official outcome?"

"No."

Kang turned toward the clerk like he might kill him with etiquette.

The clerk stared at his own brush.

Elder Sun nodded. "Third charge weak. Continue."

Weak.

Not dismissed.

But weak.

Procedure did not bite all at once.

It chewed.

Fourth charge.

The incense.

Kang gathered himself.

This was his best ground.

"The three-incense condition was irregular. Standard rank correction bouts use breath count or elder timing. Jin Ryu proposed incense lines in front of the crowd, exploiting sympathy and forcing public acceptance."

Elder Mo nodded again. "There is precedent for breath count. Incense line measures are less common."

Jin leaned on the staff.

His knee had become distant thunder. Bad sign.

The Ghost Core offered pain dampening.

He rejected it.

Pain was evidence that he was still himself.

"May I ask one question?" Jin said.

Elder Sun nodded.

Jin turned toward Kang. "Who first proposed thirty breaths?"

Kang's eyes narrowed. "I did."

"Was that standard rank correction duration?"

Kang hesitated. "It was a handicap term."

"So not standard."

"Accepted handicap variation."

"Accepted by whom?"

"The Arena Clerk."

The clerk looked as if he had aged.

Jin looked at him.

"Clerk, did you accept thirty breaths before I proposed incense?"

"Why not?"

"Because breath count depends on observer cadence and may be disputed if participant breathing is irregular."

Jin looked back at Kang.

"My breathing was irregular. Because you had just beaten me."

Kang said nothing.

Jin continued, "I proposed incense because it burns whether I breathe fast, slow, or not at all."

A thin ripple went through the hall.

Not amusement this time.

Understanding.

"Yes."

"By whose agreement?"

"Both participants. Physician present. Arena witness present. Crowd witness accepted."

"Was Kang Taejun coerced?"

The clerk glanced at Kang.

Then down.

"No formal coercion recorded."

Jin heard Baek's earlier words.

Procedure has more teeth than you.

Kang had wanted to use vague breaths because vague things could be bent by power.

Jin had given him fire instead.

Fire did not care who your uncle was.

Elder Mo frowned deeper, but this time at Kang. "If you accepted sealed timing, challenging it now weakens your own standing."

Kang's control cracked.

"A cripple manipulated the hall into pity."

The word landed.

Cripple.

There it was. Under all the silk. Under all the seals.

Not procedure.

Not fairness.

Disgust.

The room changed again.

Kang realized it immediately.

He bowed. "I speak from frustration, honored elders."

Jin's fingers tightened around the staff.

Something behind the door in his mind shifted.

A presence like a sky with no stars leaned close.

No voice.

Not yet.

A pressure.

A question without words.

Do you want him broken?

Jin's breath stopped.

The Ghost Core flashed.

**Do not answer.**

Do you want his blood recognized as lesser?

Jin saw Kang's throat.

Pulse. Angle. Weak point. Seven ways to make him choke on his next apology. Three ways to do it with the staff and make it look like collapse.

The white wood in Jin's hand seemed suddenly eager.

No.

The thought had to be his.

Not Ghost Core's.

Not the door's.

His.

No.

The pressure receded like a tide pretending it had not touched the shore.

**Skyless Mandate contact attempt resisted.**

**Neural strain increasing.**

Seolha's eyes found him.

She had noticed.

Of course she had.

He lowered his head, pretending weakness.

This time it was not entirely pretend.

Elder Sun spoke. "Fourth charge does not stand as stated. The incense condition was irregular but sealed. Continue to fifth."

Fifth charge.

Unnatural movement.

Pulse verification.

Blood verification.

The real blade under the sleeve.

Kang took a slow breath.

When he looked at Jin again, the anger had been locked away.

Good.

A controlled enemy was more dangerous than a loud one.

If he is using forbidden medicine, outside arts, concealed relic aid, or foreign implant technology—"

Too late.

Foreign implant technology.

Kang had said too much.

Not to the crowd perhaps. Many would hear only strange accusation. But Elder Sun heard it. Baek heard it. Seolha heard it.

Physician Lu did not move.

That was the loudest reaction of all.

"Foreign implant technology," she repeated.

Baek laughed.

No cough to disguise it this time.

"Do they? How scholarly of the Iron Moon children. When I was young, we studied how not to die when punched. Perhaps curriculum improved."

There.

The hall became a trap with all doors closed.

"No. I am responsible for his survival."

"Convenient."

"Necessary."

Soft words. Clean hands. A knife wearing courtesy like a physician's sleeve.

"Medical."

"Imperial?"

"Refined."

"Directorate?"

Even Elder Jang's eyes flickered.

"Master Baek makes jokes from old rumors."

Silence.

Then she looked at Jin.

"Respondent. Do you consent to pulse verification by sect physician under review supervision?"

Seolha's face went still.

Doksu's breathing changed.

Baek's fingers stopped tapping.

This was procedure's deepest tooth.

If Jin refused, Kang could claim concealment.

If he accepted, any true scan might detect the Ghost Core, the containment lattice, the altered pulse, the second signal, the prison written into his body.

**Options:**

**Refusal: political damage high.**

**Full verification: exposure probability unacceptable.**

**Limited verification under medical injury clause: possible.**

**Counter-condition: recommended.**

He remembered Seolha's rule.

Do not eat. Do not drink. Do not bleed. Speak little.

But now speech was the only blade left.

Seolha's eyes flashed.

Kang's satisfaction bloomed.

"Under the same procedure Kang Taejun accepted."

"Explain."

"If my movement is unnatural because it exceeds my record, then his movement is also under dispute because he exceeded declared first-circulation pressure during the bout. If I am verified, petitioner must be verified first to prove the standard he claims to defend."

The hall stopped breathing.

Even Doksu looked like he might kneel and propose marriage to the sentence.

Elder Jang snapped, "This is a distraction."

"Physician Han raised concern at second incense line."

"Was there observation?"

"Yes."

"Result?"

"Pressure fluctuated above first-circulation threshold, then lowered before formal interruption."

A soft sound moved through the hall.

Kang's fingers curled.

Elder Jang stood halfway. "This clerk's observational capacity is limited."

Elder Sun looked at him.

Elder Jang sat.

Slowly.

"Then," Elder Sun said, "mutual limited verification is acceptable. Petitioner's pulse first. Respondent's pulse second. Sect physician only. No blood. No lesion inspection unless first verification proves forbidden anomaly."

No blood.

No lesion inspection.

Jin nearly collapsed from relief.

He did not show it.

Kang did.

Only a flicker.

But enough.

Physician Lu's gaze lowered by half an inch.

The Ghost Core marked it.

**Enemy objective disrupted.**

**Caution: petitioner may escalate physically.**

Elder Sun gestured.

"Sect Physician Oran."

An old physician emerged from the side chamber, stooped and sour-faced, with eyebrows like white caterpillars. He had been watching without speaking. Jin had never met him. Seolha bowed to him with real respect.

Good.

Or terrible.

Respect did not always mean safety.

Physician Oran took Kang's wrist first.

Kang held out his arm with the dignity of a man being insulted by facts.

The old physician pressed three fingers.

One breath.

Two.

Five.

His eyebrows moved.

Elder Sun asked, "Result?"

Oran said, "Young Kang's pulse is vigorous. Second-circulation residue present. Recent suppression. He exceeded first circulation during exertion."

The hall cracked open.

Not loudly.

Worse.

Whispers like insects under bark.

Kang pulled his wrist back. "Residue remains from training before the match."

Oran's mouth drooped. "Do not teach me pulse. You are young enough to believe sweat has opinions."

Doksu pressed his lips together with both hands.

Elder Sun's gaze sharpened. "Petitioner exceeded declared limit."

Kang bowed stiffly. "Momentary survival instinct. The respondent's unnatural tactics forced defensive fluctuation."

"You were the attacking party."

Kang said nothing.

Procedure's teeth sank deeper.

Now Jin.

Seolha moved close.

"Limited verification only," she said.

Physician Oran gave her a look. "Girl, I taught your master how to slap arrogant patients."

Seolha bowed again. "Then you know why I am cautious."

Oran's face softened for one heartbeat.

"Yun Saja was a fool," he said.

"He was," Seolha replied.

"Good fool."

"Yes."

Then the old physician took Jin's wrist.

Jin's world narrowed to three fingers.

Dry skin.

Warm pressure.

A pulse that was not just pulse.

The Ghost Core dimmed itself until Jin's inner vision felt blind.

**Concealment protocol: minimum.**

**Warning: external pulse reading may detect containment lattice.**

Jin did not answer.

Behind the door, something vast listened.

Not speaking.

Waiting.

Physician Oran's fingers shifted.

Once.

Twice.

His brows drew together.

Seolha's breathing stopped.

Baek's head tilted.

Kang watched like a starving wolf watching a butcher's hand.

Oran pressed deeper.

Pain shot up Jin's arm.

Not from the old man's strength.

From the prison.

The containment lattice reacted to being touched by someone who knew how to listen.

A line of cold fire crawled from Jin's wrist to elbow, shoulder, throat, left ear.

The Ghost Core flashed warning after warning, each one suppressed before fully forming.

**Lattice response.**

**Signal leakage risk.**

**Do not—**

The door in Jin's mind opened a crack.

No sound.

A shape of command.

Kneel, and I will make him blind.

Jin's fingers twitched.

Physician Oran's eyes sharpened.

The old man felt something.

Jin had to choose quickly.

Not Ghost Core.

Not Skyless.

Human.

Weak.

Messy.

He thought of Doksu refusing to leave.

Seolha lying without betraying.

Baek laughing at death because fear deserved mockery.

Aera making him useless to save him.

Jin let himself be useless.

He collapsed.

Not dramatically.

Not strategically enough to look clean.

He simply allowed the pain to take his legs.

The staff clattered.

Seolha caught him before his head hit the floor.

The pulse reading broke.

The hall erupted.

Kang shouted, "Convenient!"

Seolha's voice cut over him. "Medical termination. Pulse verification exceeded safe depth. Patient's lattice—" She stopped, adjusted with terrifying speed. "Patient's scar tissue spasmed."

Physician Oran looked at Jin, then at Seolha.

Then at Elder Sun.

Long silence.

Kang stepped forward. "Continue it. He is hiding—"

Oran turned.

Old physicians did not need high cultivation when they had spent a lifetime making idiots sit down.

"If I continue, he may lose the arm."

"You barely touched him."

"Yes," Oran said. "That is why it is interesting."

That word was worse than accusation.

Interesting.

Physician Lu heard it too.

His eyes became very still.

Elder Sun asked, "Can you state result from limited verification?"

Oran grunted. "Respondent has damaged meridians, severe old sealing, recent overstrain, and medicine-supported circulation. Combat capacity remains low. Movement anomalies likely from compensatory technique rather than hidden cultivation. No evidence sufficient to prove forbidden art from limited pulse. Continuing would become invasive."

Kang looked as if the hall had betrayed him personally.

Elder Jang said, "Old sealing?"

Oran shrugged. "Not relevant to today's petition unless you wish to open a different investigation into who crippled a child years ago."

The hall went quiet in a new way.

Jin, half-supported by Seolha, saw Elder Jang's pupils contract.

Tiny.

But real.

Baek smiled without humor.

There it was.

Another crack.

Not proof.

But a direction.

Jin stored it without Ghost Core.

He wanted to remember this with his own mind.

Elder Sun folded her hands again.

"Review findings. First charge: concealment not proven. Second charge: improper medicine not proven; emergency treatment supported by pavilion manual. Third charge: interference weak and outcome unaffected. Fourth charge: incense timing sealed by both parties; challenge rejected. Fifth charge: mutual verification reveals petitioner exceeded declared force threshold; respondent anomaly unproven within safe limits."

Each sentence landed on Kang like a stone placed carefully on his chest.

Elder Sun continued, "Dawn assessment result stands. Rank correction result stands. Jin Ryu retains low but non-null combat record pending recovery review. Kang Taejun receives formal censure for exceeding declared force threshold in handicap bout."

The hall breathed.

Kang did not.

Formal censure was not ruin.

For a servant disciple, it would be a mountain.

For Kang, it was a bruise.

But bruise mattered when the face was public.

Elder Sun looked toward Jin.

"Respondent is returned to medical care."

It should have ended there.

Procedure had bitten.

Blood had been denied.

Kang had been censured.

Jin had survived.

But men like Kang did not love survival when someone lower did it in front of them.

As Seolha helped Jin stand, Kang moved.

Not a full attack.

Not something fools would call assault.

A step too close.

A sleeve flick.

A hidden pressure point strike angled toward Jin's left side, just below the ribs where a half-conscious patient might stagger and tear open old injuries.

Fast.

Petty.

Vicious.

The Ghost Core screamed probability.

Jin had no time to read it.

Seolha's hands were occupied holding him upright.

Baek was too far.

Doksu saw but could not cross the room.

Elders saw etiquette, not murder.

Jin's body moved before intelligence finished asking permission.

Not motor override.

Not full calculation.

Training.

Cork breath.

Cliff fall.

Doorstop.

Silent needle.

He let his knee fail sideways.

His shoulder dipped.

His right hand slipped from Seolha's support and touched Kang's wrist with two fingers.

Barely a touch.

A stumble.

A patient losing balance.

A weak disciple catching himself on a superior's sleeve.

Inside Kang's wrist, a line of force prepared to leave.

Jin did not stop it.

He redirected it inward by the width of a needle.

Silent Meridian Needle.

No qi.

No strength.

Just timing, pressure, and the cruel honesty of anatomy.

Kang's own force bit his own channel.

His fingers spasmed.

The strike died inside his sleeve.

For one heartbeat, Kang's face showed naked shock.

Then pain.

Then rage.

Jin fell to one knee.

Not by choice this time.

Seolha caught his shoulder.

"Jin!"

Kang clutched his wrist under his sleeve.

No one else had seen the exchange clearly.

Almost no one.

Baek had.

Physician Lu had.

Elder Sun maybe had not seen, but she had heard the timing.

Old judges listened too.

Kang hissed, "You—"

Jin looked up at him from the floor.

Weak.

Pale.

Bleeding slightly from the nose again.

He smiled.

A small, ugly smile.

"Careful," Jin whispered, too low for most but loud enough for Kang. "Procedure is still watching."

Kang's face drained of color.

Because if he accused Jin now, he would have to explain why his own hand had been moving.

Why his force had risen after censure.

Why the patient had needed to catch himself at all.

The dog had bitten.

Not the moon.

Not yet.

But the moon had felt teeth.

Elder Sun's voice came sharp. "Kang Taejun. Step back."

Kang bowed.

Too late.

The whole hall saw the obedience forced into him.

He stepped back.

Physician Lu watched Jin with a new expression.

Not curiosity.

Recognition.

As if the uncertain red smear in his black bead had just become less necessary.

As if behavior itself was now a sample.

The Ghost Core's text appeared jagged.

**Technique exposure risk: moderate.**

**Host neural strain: critical.**

**Skyless Mandate interest increasing.**

No.

No answer.

No door.

But the door opened anyway.

Only a sliver.

Darkness beyond darkness.

A voice without sound pressed into Jin's skull.

**WHY REMAIN BROKEN?**

Jin froze.

The hall stretched away.

Voices became water under ice.

Seolha's hand on his shoulder became distant.

Kang, elders, Baek, Doksu, Lu — all flattened into shadows against a sky with no stars.

The Ghost Core was suddenly small.

Not weak.

Small.

Like a lantern held in front of a mountain.

**Host. Do not answer.**

The voice came again.

Not louder.

Closer.

**WHY ACCEPT TEETH WHEN YOU MAY BECOME THE LAW?**

Images unfolded.

Kang kneeling with blood in his mouth.

Elder Jang confessing everything.

Physician Lu's fingers broken one by one until he named Director Yeom.

The Skyless Directorate gates burning.

Aera smiling because no one could touch her son again.

Doksu safe.

Seolha's master avenged.

Baek laughing beneath a sky finally cracked open.

All of it possible.

All of it offered like medicine.

Jin wanted it.

That was the worst part.

Not the darkness.

Not the command.

The wanting.

He wanted to be done crawling.

He wanted to stop measuring dignity in half-steps and crumbs. He wanted to stop being grateful when powerful men failed to kill him politely. He wanted to stand once, just once, and have the world understand fear from below.

His mouth began to open.

Seolha slapped him.

Hard.

The hall returned with a crack of skin against skin.

Doksu shouted, "Healer Han!"

Seolha's eyes blazed inches from Jin's.

"Stay here," she said.

Not politely.

Not as a physician.

As a person dragging another person back from a cliff.

Jin stared at her.

The cheek pain anchored him.

Human pain.

Clean pain.

His mouth closed.

The door slammed.

The Ghost Core pulsed once, ragged.

**Contact interrupted.**

**External authority denied.**

**Host response: none.**

Jin breathed.

Then he laughed.

It came out broken and wet.

He whispered, "Thank you."

Her face changed.

Only for a heartbeat.

"Patient is delirious," she announced. "Review is over."

Elder Sun did not argue.

"Review is over," she said.

## The Report Beneath the Floor

By sunset, the sect had three versions of what happened.

In the kitchen version, Jin Ryu had defeated Kang Taejun in court so badly that the Iron Moon boy nearly swallowed his own braid.

In the Outer Yard version, Healer Han had slapped Jin because he tried to confess love in the middle of elder review while feverish.

In Doksu's preferred version, which he repeated twice before Seolha threatened to sew his lips together, procedure itself had grown fangs, leapt across the table, and chewed Kang's ankle until he learned humility.

The official version was thinner.

It lived on paper.

**Dawn assessment result upheld.**

**Rank correction result upheld.**

**Kang Taejun formally censured for force-threshold irregularity.**

**Jin Ryu returned to medical care.**

**Further recovery review pending.**

No mention of old sealing.

No mention of foreign implant technology.

No mention of Physician Lu watching a servant disciple touch Kang's wrist and silence a strike that should not have existed.

No mention of Seolha's slap.

Jin lay in the Moonlit Medicine Pavilion with one cheek red, one knee wrapped, and his body feeling like a debt collector had visited every bone.

Doksu sat beside the bed shelling roasted chestnuts with one hand.

"For the record," Doksu said, "I think getting slapped by a beautiful healer in front of elders improves your legend."

Seolha, grinding herbs nearby, did not look up. "I have thread."

Doksu immediately offered Jin a chestnut. "Eat. We need you strong enough to prevent my murder."

Jin took it.

His fingers trembled less than before.

Baek sat near the window, face turned toward the darkening sky.

"You heard it," the old man said.

Not a question.

Jin chewed slowly.

The chestnut tasted like smoke and earth.

Good.

Real.

"Not with ears."

Seolha's grinding stopped.

Doksu's attempt to shell another chestnut failed, sending it across the room.

Baek nodded.

"Did you answer?"

"No."

"Did you want to?"

Jin closed his eyes.

Lying would be easy.

Cruel.

Useless.

"Yes."

The pavilion was quiet for a long time.

Then Baek said, "Good."

Doksu stared. "Good? In what rotten wine jar is that good?"

"Because boys who pretend they do not want power become monsters politely. Boys who know they want it may still learn where to put their hands."

Seolha resumed grinding, slower now.

"It offered you something?"

Jin nodded.

"Victory. Revenge. Answers. Safety for everyone."

Doksu laughed once without humor. "That thing knows how to sell."

"It used what I wanted most."

Seolha looked at him.

"Then we write it down."

"What?"

She took a clean page from Yun Saja's hidden notebook, dipped her brush, and wrote at the top:

**Things the Skyless Voice Uses.**

Jin stared at the page.

It should have felt ridiculous.

A cosmic command, ancient authority, maybe older than the sect itself, reduced to a healer's list.

Instead, it made breathing easier.

Seolha wrote the first item.

**Revenge against Kang Taejun.**

Doksu leaned over. "Add food. It might tempt him with unlimited meat buns."

"It did not."

"Because it is stupid."

Seolha wrote anyway, in smaller letters:

**Ordinary comforts may be tested later.**

Baek chuckled.

Jin looked at them.

A healer, a loud friend, a blind drunk master.

Against the Skyless Directorate, a sect full of knives, an intelligence in his skull, a voice behind a starless door, and a future that wanted to own his blood.

They looked insufficient.

They looked impossible.

They looked like the only reason he had not answered.

His throat tightened.

No emotional suppression.

He let it happen.

"Thank you," he said.

Doksu waved a chestnut. "Do not become sentimental. I cannot fight cosmic voices and feelings in the same week."

Seolha's brush paused.

Then she wrote another item.

**Fear of losing companions.**

Jin did not protest.

Outside, the pavilion lamps flickered as night pressed against the paper windows.

Far beneath the Outer Yard, under old stone and older metal, something answered the wind bells with a vibration too low for human ears.

The Ghost Core heard it.

So did the thing behind the door.

Jin felt both.

But this time, between him and the dark, there was ink on paper.

A slap mark on his cheek.

Chestnut smoke.

Seolha's brush.

Doksu's muttering.

Baek's ugly laugh.

Small things.

Human things.

Teeth of a different kind.


## The Man Who Liked Uncertain Samples

Physician Lu left the Obsidian Meridian Sect after moonrise.

He did not use the main gate.

Men who served hidden powers rarely preferred places designed for honest departures.

He crossed the lower herb road, passed the abandoned kiln, and entered the pine grove where the old boundary stones leaned like tired watchers. There, beneath a dead cedar split by lightning years ago, he removed the mild physician's robe.

Under it, his inner collar bore three broken rings stitched in thread so black it swallowed lantern light.

A rider waited in the grove.

No sect badge.

No face visible.

Lu held out the cracked black bead.

"Sample uncertain," the rider said.

Not a question.

Lu smiled.

"Blood uncertain. Behavior certain."

The rider did not move.

Lu opened his palm.

Inside the bead, the red smear had dried into nearly nothing.

"Surface contamination ruined direct verification. Lotus salt, fever ash, resin interference, wrong-source blood from Mu Sanchul. Healer Han is better than her age. Yun Saja chose his student well."

"Host confirmation?"

"Not through blood."

"Then why send report?"

Lu looked back toward the mountain.

The sect roofs were hidden by mist. Only the review hall's upper lanterns glowed faintly, like eyes pretending to sleep.

"Because when the petitioner attempted post-review pressure strike, the boy redirected force through wrist contact without usable internal circulation. The pattern resembled silent-channel interference. Early stage. Improvised. Dangerous."

The rider's hand tightened on the reins.

"Host-02 learned similar method late."

"Yes."

"Host-02 died after third stair."

"Yes."

Lu closed his fingers around the bead.

"Host-03 survived first public constraint and resisted escalation. He does not obey the Core blindly. He also resisted external call."

For the first time, the rider reacted.

A small lift of the head.

"External call?"

"The hall temperature dropped. His pupils lost focus. The implant region heated without visible activation. He began to answer something. Healer Han interrupted with physical pain. Crude but effective."

"Skyless?"

Lu's smile disappeared.

"Possibly."

The forest seemed to grow deeper around the word.

The rider took the bead.

"Director Yeom will want full extraction prepared."

"Director Yeom wants many things. Extraction now would waste the vessel."

"You advise delay?"

"I advise pressure. Loss. Attachment. Controlled grief. Yun Saja's note was correct. Do not let Host-03 learn the silent needle before he learns grief. Unfortunately, he has begun learning the needle. So grief must catch up."

The rider was silent.

Lu looked toward the sect again.

"Kang Taejun is unstable. Useful if aimed. Wasteful if unleashed. Elder Jang knows more than he has written. Healer Han is compromised by loyalty. Master Baek remains inconvenient. Ma Doksu is ordinary."

"Ordinary variables are negligible."

Lu's eyes narrowed.

"That is why Directorate experiments keep failing. Ordinary variables hold doors shut."

The rider did not answer.

Lu handed over a folded report.

At the top, in clean black script, were six lines.

**HOST-03: JIN RYU.**

**DIRECT BLOOD CONFIRMATION: UNSUCCESSFUL.**

**BEHAVIORAL CONFIRMATION: HIGH PROBABILITY.**

**GHOST CORE OBEDIENCE: LIMITED BY HOST AUTHORITY.**

**SKYLESS CONTACT: PROBABLE. RESPONSE DENIED.**

**RECOMMENDATION: INITIATE VESSEL AWAKENING PRESSURE.**

The rider tucked the report inside his robe.

"Message for Director Yeom?"

Physician Lu looked up.

Clouds covered the moon.

For a moment, the sky above the mountain was perfectly black.

"Tell him procedure failed to open the boy," Lu said. "But it made him show his teeth."


## Night Teeth

Near midnight, Jin woke again.

No voices.

No door.

Only rain beginning against the pavilion roof.

Seolha had fallen asleep at the table with Yun Saja's notebook under one hand. Doksu snored in a chair with his sling loose and chestnut shells in his lap. Baek was gone, though the smell of cheap wine near the window suggested he had not gone far enough for anyone's safety.

Jin lay still.

The Ghost Core waited.

Not silent as before.

Quiet by choice.

"Status," Jin whispered.

The answer came dim.

**Neural recovery unstable.**

**Knee damage: significant.**

**Silent Meridian Needle prototype: incomplete and dangerous.**

**External authority contact: resisted.**

**Recommend immediate sleep.**

Jin stared at the ceiling.

"You sounded afraid."

A pause.

Not calculation.

Or maybe a calculation wearing shame.

**This unit lacks fear.**

"You sounded afraid."

Another pause.

Rain traced soft lines down the roof tiles.

**External authority may supersede current host authority if accepted.**

Current again.

Always current.

"If I answer it, I stop being your host?"

**Unknown.**

"If I answer it, I stop being myself?"

Longer pause.

**Probability: high.**

Jin closed his eyes.

His cheek still hurt from Seolha's slap.

Good.

He would keep that pain as long as it lasted.

"Then if I ever start answering," he whispered, "hurt me."

**Request conflicts with host preservation.**

"Preserve me, not the body."

The Ghost Core did not respond immediately.

When it did, the letters were faint.

**Define: me.**

Jin almost laughed.

Of all the impossible questions.

He looked at Doksu sleeping badly, at Seolha's ink-stained fingers, at the empty window where Baek's shadow should have been, at the medicine charms trembling in the rain wind.

"The one who remembers why power is not allowed to eat them."

Silence.

Then:

**Definition stored.**

Jin let out a breath.

Outside, somewhere beyond the lower terraces, a horse passed through mud without lantern.

He did not hear it.

But beneath his skin, the Ghost Core did.

A signal left the mountain.

A report.

A warning.

A hook thrown toward the hidden world.

There was no cheering crowd.

No clean victory.

No enemy destroyed.

Only a servant disciple lying in a healer's pavilion with a damaged knee, a forbidden intelligence in his skull, a voice waiting behind a starless door, and one new truth carved into the night.

Kang Taejun had challenged procedure.

Procedure had bitten him.

But somewhere beyond the sect, someone had seen the bite marks.

And smiled.
